<h4><b>Matteo Grazioso</b></h4>
<h6><i>Ph.D. Student in Computer Science @ Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i></h6>
<h6><i>Visiting Ph.D. Student in Computer Science @ University of Milano-Bicocca</i></h6>
<h6><i>Scientific Associate @ Italian National Institute of Nuclear Physics (INFN), Milano Bicocca Division</i></h6>

<p>
Matteo Grazioso is a <b>Ph.D. Student in Computer Science</b> at <i>Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i> (Department of Environmental Sciences, Informatics and Statistics). His primary research focuses on <b>Interpretable and Fairness-Preserving AI</b> for high-risk applications, exploring the design of <b>trustworthy AI systems</b> to ensure that machine learning models in high-stakes domains are transparent, reliable, and ethically aligned.
</p>

<p>
His research interests span <b>Computational Intelligence, Machine Learning, Interpretable AI, High-Performance Computing, Evolutionary Drug Discovery</b>, and <b>Optimization Algorithms</b>. Matteo holds a Master’s Degree in <i>Computer Science – Artificial Intelligence and Data Engineering</i>, graduating <b>summa cum laude</b> from <i>Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i>, where he also contributed as a Research Grant Holder.
</p>

<p>
Alongside his doctoral studies, Matteo is a <b>Scientific Associate</b> at the <b>Italian National Institute of Nuclear Physics (INFN)</b>, Milano Bicocca Division, and a <b>Visiting Ph.D. Student</b> at the <i>University of Milano-Bicocca</i> (Department of Informatics, Systems and Communication). He is an active member of the <b>IEEE</b> and the <b>IEEE Computational Intelligence Society (CIS)</b>, contributing to the <i>CIS Task Force on Advanced Representation in Biological and Medical Search and Optimization</i>.
</p>

<p>
Matteo’s overarching goal is to advance the development of <b>trustworthy, interpretable, and ethically aligned AI systems</b>, particularly in domains where AI’s societal impact is significant, such as <b>healthcare and biomedical research</b>.
</p>
