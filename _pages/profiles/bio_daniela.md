<h4><b>Daniela Besozzi</b></h4>
<h6><i>Associate Professor @ University of Milano-Bicocca</i></h6>

<p>
Daniela Besozzi is <b>Associate Professor</b> at the <i>Department of Informatics, Systems and Communication</i> at the <b>University of Milano-Bicocca</b>, Italy, where she is also affiliated with the interdepartmental research centres <b>“Bicocca Bioinformatics Biostatistics and Bioimaging Centre – B4”</b> and <b>“Bicocca Research Centre in Health Services – BReCHS”</b>.
</p>

<p>
Her research is mainly focused on the <b>mathematical modelling of complex biological systems</b> – with a special interest in the cellular processes that lead to the onset and progression of <i>multifactorial diseases</i> – and the development of <b>bioinformatics and artificial intelligence methods</b> in medical disciplines, ranging from <b>oncology to digital pathology</b>. Her work contributed, among the others, to the identification of therapeutic targets in cancer cells and the screening for combination cancer therapies by means of <b>fuzzy logic modelling</b> and <b>multi-objective optimization algorithms</b>; the generation of a novel protocol to study cell proliferation in human acute myeloid leukemia xenografts by means of <b>stochastic modelling</b> and <b>swarm intelligence meta-heuristics</b>; and the development of a <b>deep learning-based method</b> for the morphometric characterization and multiclass segmentation of nuclei in thyroid lesions.
</p>

<p>
Daniela is co-author of <b>more than 100 peer-reviewed works</b> published in international journals, conference proceedings, and book chapters. She is <b>co-inventor of a patented and CE-marked method</b> to automatically predict the optimal patient-specific inversion time for late gadolinium enhancement imaging in <i>cardiac magnetic resonance</i>. She serves as <b>associate editor</b> for <i>Frontiers in Systems Biology</i>, section on <i>Multiscale Mechanistic Modeling</i>, and she is member of the <b>IEEE CIS Task Force</b> on “Advanced representation in biological and medical search and optimization”.
</p>

<p>
Daniela is an active member of the <b>working group on gender equality</b> at the University of Milano-Bicocca and contributed to editing the <b>Gender Equality Plan (GEP)</b>. She is co-promoter of a pilot project to incentivize the <b>enrolment of female students in STEM disciplines</b>, and she regularly contributes to the organization of events related to the dissemination of knowledge, curiosity and passion about science and literature to the broader society. She has two cats.
</p>
