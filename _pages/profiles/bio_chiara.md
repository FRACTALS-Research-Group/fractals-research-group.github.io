Chiara Gallese graduated in <b>East Asian Languages, Economics, and Law</b> from <i>Ca' Foscari University</i> and earned her <b>PhD in Asian Studies</b> from the same university in 2017 with a thesis on private international law in Japan, which was awarded the <b>Bonacossa Prize</b> for best doctoral thesis. She also holds a <b>law degree</b> from the <i>University of Padua</i>. In 2023, she was awarded the <b>European Marie Skłodowska Curie Postdoctoral Fellowship</b> in personal data protection.

She is currently a <b>visiting lecturer</b> for the <i>"Digital Ethics"</i> course at the International College of Ca' Foscari. For Ca' Foscari, she also contributed to drafting the <b>guidelines on the use of artificial intelligence in research</b>.

She is a member of the <b>European Commission's working groups</b> for the drafting of the two <i>Codes of Conduct on Artificial Intelligence</i> and of the <b>IEEE technical committees</b> for education on the ethics and legal aspects of artificial intelligence. She also sits on the <b>ethics committees</b> of several European projects.

She is an <b>Associate Editor</b> of the <i>IEEE Transactions on Artificial Intelligence</i>, an area editor of <i>Data & Policy</i> (Cambridge University Press), an international editor of the <i>Journal of Digital Technologies and Law</i>, and a member of the editorial board of the journal <i>Genius</i>. In 2022, she won the <b>best paper award</b> at the <i>IEEE CIBCB conference</i> for an article on the <b>ethics of data reuse</b>.

During her career, she has obtained the <b>bar exam</b> and the certification as an <b>international privacy expert (CIPP/E)</b>. She served on the regulatory committee of the <i>Eindhoven Medtec Innovation Centre</i> and worked as a <b>privacy expert</b> at the <i>University of Eindhoven</i>. She has also lectured on artificial intelligence at several European universities and institutions.

In 2024, she participated as a speaker at a <b>TEDx event</b> on artificial intelligence, presenting the <b>FanFAIR software</b> for the semi-automatic analysis of the fairness of datasets, of which she is co-creator.
