<h4><b>Davide Casarin</b></h4>
<h6><i>Bachelor’s Thesis Student @ Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i></h6>

<p>
Davide Casarin is a final-year <b>Bachelor’s student in Data Science</b> at <i>Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i>, where he has developed a strong foundation in <b>mathematical modeling, statistical learning</b>, and <b>computational methods</b> for data-intensive science.
</p>

<p>
He is currently conducting his Bachelor’s thesis research at <i>University of Milano-Bicocca</i> in collaboration with <b>INFN</b> and <i>Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i> within a <b>PNRR-funded research initiative</b>. His work focuses on the <b>numerical analysis and GPU-accelerated implementation of stochastic differential equations (SDEs)</b> for optimization in physics-based models. By integrating <i>stochastic modeling, high-performance computing, and parallel architectures</i>, his research aims to advance scalable computational methodologies for complex dynamical systems.
</p>

<p>
His academic interests lie at the intersection of <b>probability theory, optimization, and machine learning</b>, with particular attention to theoretically grounded methods that translate into robust and efficient large-scale implementations.
</p>

<p>
In parallel with his academic trajectory, he has gained several years of professional experience as a <b>Software Developer</b>, working on <b>cloud-native and mobile systems</b>. His industry experience has strengthened his expertise in <b>scalable software architecture, performance engineering</b>, and the design of maintainable, production-grade systems—skills that directly inform his approach to <i>scientific computing and research software development</i>.
</p>
