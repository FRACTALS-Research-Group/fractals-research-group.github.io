<h4><b>Silvia Multari</b></h4>
<h6><i>Ph.D. Candidate in Science and Technology of Bio and Nano Materials @ Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i></h6>

<p>
Silvia Multari is a <b>Ph.D. Candidate in Science and Technology of Bio and Nano Materials</b> at the <i>Ca’ Foscari University of Venice</i>, Department of Molecular Sciences and Nanosystems. She holds a bachelor’s degree in <b>Pharmaceutical Biotechnology</b> obtained at the <i>University of Milan</i>, and she is now specialising in <b>computational applications to drug discovery</b>, with a focus on the study of molecular interactions, combining <i>physics-based and AI-driven approaches</i> to investigate peptide and small-molecule binding.
</p>

<p>
She was a <b>visiting student</b> at the <i>Technical University of Eindhoven</i> (Eindhoven, the Netherlands) in the <b>Molecular Machine Learning group</b> led by professor Francesca Grisoni, and then at the <i>Kyoto Institute of Technology</i> (Kyoto, Japan) under the supervision of professor Giuseppe Pezzotti. Her main expertise lies in the application of <b>molecular dynamics simulations, molecular docking</b> and <b>machine learning</b>.
</p>
