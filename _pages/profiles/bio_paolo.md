<h4><b>Paolo Cazzaniga</b></h4>
<h6><i>Associate Professor @ University of Bergamo</i></h6>

<p>
Paolo Cazzaniga is <b>Associate Professor</b> at the <i>University of Bergamo</i>, Italy.
His research is located in the multidisciplinary field of <b>Systems Biology</b>. His activities range from the definition of <b>mathematical models of complex (biological) systems</b> to the simulation of their temporal dynamics by means of <i>deterministic, stochastic or hybrid algorithms</i>. He also defines and applies different methodologies for the analysis of the <b>emergent behavior</b> of the systems under investigation (e.g., <i>parameter sweep analysis, parameter identifiability, sensitivity analysis</i>).
</p>

<p>
His research effort also concerns the definition, implementation (for <b>CPUs and GPUs</b>) and application of <b>Artificial Intelligence methods</b> to problems in the fields of <b>Bioinformatics, Systems Biology and Biomedicine</b>, like, for instance, inferring the full haplotype of a cell starting from read sequencing data, estimating the unknown information of a complex system (e.g., molecular concentrations and kinetic constants), and enhancing the appearance and visual quality of <b>biomedical images</b>.
</p>

<p>
He has already successfully applied different methods, such as <b>Particle Swarm Optimization</b>, <b>Genetic Algorithms</b>, and <b>Deep Neural Networks</b>, to these problems.
</p>
